Education is the Silver Bullet.

---

The lights dim. The band stops. I start speaking. When I stop the name readers start and graduation photos flash accross the big screen. Graduating with the "Toro Friendship Award" and as "Outstanding English Student of the Year" couldn't replace the 7 ceramic pots I hadn't been able to finish after spring break or the national We The People Tournament that I competed in from my bedroom instead of Washington D.C but reflecting on 4 years at Mountain View High School helped me look forward with optimism towards my upcoming experience at Brigham Young University.

Empty hallways, zoom classes and T.A office hours. I experienced my first year at BYU through a mask. After my first year I deferred to volunteer as a missionary for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints in Mendoza, Argentina. Masks became barbijos, the pandemic became la pandemia, but as soon as I began to decipher spanish through a mouthful of fabric the mandates lifted, the vaccine was available to everyone, and communicating became easier.

Walking the halls of BYU as a sophomore I still find myself wondering why the hallways are so crowded and why I can't participate in class from my apartment. But as soon as I sit down in a classroom and exchange glances with my classmates my questions are answered; connection. Helping each other understand a difficult lecture, swapping tips about summer internships, campus employment, and student housing. Laughing as we realize none of us know what we're doing no matter how long we've been alive. My education teaches me to connect and value people. Education may be everything, but connection makes everything worthwhile.
